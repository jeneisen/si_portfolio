But there's more

background

After two years working for startups and creeping closer to the engineering table everyday, I began to teach myself to code. Less than two weeks into this programming foray, I knew becoming a builder was the right path.

While majoring in Linguistics and Spanish at the University of Michigan, I began to burn the midnight oil looking for ways to combine programming into my studies where I was introduced to computational linguistics and natural language processing. Wanting to really boost my backend skills, I moved to New York to attend the Flatiron School and quickly fell in love with ruby.

The Flatiron School changed my life and taught me to love being a beginner, because there is so much to learn. And that is exhilirating. Besides developing you can find me cooking up a healthy storm, strumming my ukulele, kickboxing and meandering the streets with absolutely no destination in mind.

Now just a few weeks away from graduation, I'm gearing up to move back to NYC. I believe work and life should be one in the same. I feel so grateful to have found what I love to do and that I get to do it everyday.
